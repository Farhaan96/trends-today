## Diseases Where CRISPR Shows Promise

**1. Sickle Cell Disease**
[CRISPR-edited stem cells](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-approves-first-gene-therapies-treat-patients-sickle-cell-disease) have achieved exceptional success rates in clinical trials, with [97% of patients experiencing complete elimination of debilitating crises](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-approves-first-gene-therapies-treat-patients-sickle-cell-disease). This represents a functional cure for most recipients.

**2. Huntington's Disease**
CRISPR research for Huntington's disease is still in [early clinical stages](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-024-02178-3), with no published cure rates yet. The field shows promise but requires more research.

**3. Cystic Fibrosis**
CRISPR trials for cystic fibrosis are [ongoing but still in early stages](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-024-02178-3), with no reported high success rates comparable to sickle cell disease yet.
